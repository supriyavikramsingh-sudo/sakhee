# Regional Meal Planning Tips for PCOS

## Andhra Pradesh
- Focus on gongura (sorrel leaves) for iron
- Use ragi regularly for calcium
- Include pesarattu for high protein
- Moderate use of spices; reduce red chili if inflammation-prone
- Tamarind-based curries aid digestion

## Karnataka
- Utilize ragi mudde as staple
- Include jowar rotis for North Karnataka meals
- Bisi bele bath (millet version) for comfort food
- Coastal Karnataka: Focus on fish for omega-3
- Use coconut moderately for healthy fats

## Kerala
- Coconut-based curries provide healthy fats
- Appam and puttu are steamed, low-fat options
- Focus on fish and seafood for protein
- Use coconut oil in moderation
- Buttermilk-based curries aid digestion

## Tamil Nadu
- Include idli-sambar as regular breakfast
- Ragi koozh for cooling effect
- Adai provides high protein
- Use curd rice for probiotic benefits
- Focus on rasam for digestion

## Telangana
- Similar to Andhra, with Hyderabadi influence
- Include millets regularly
- Bagara baingan for special occasions
- Focus on protein-rich pesarattu
- Use gongura for iron content

## Puducherry
- Blend of Tamil and French influences
- Focus on seafood for protein
- Include fresh salads (French influence)
- Lighter preparations compared to other regions
- Fresh coastal vegetables

## Lakshadweep
- Tuna and seafood as primary protein
- Fresh coconut in most meals
- Limited vegetarian options naturally
- Focus on fish for omega-3
- Simple, fresh preparations

# General Guidelines Across All States

1. **Portion Control**: 60g brown rice or 70-80g millets per meal
2. **Protein Priority**: Include 100-150g protein source per meal
3. **Healthy Fats**: Use coconut, peanuts, ghee in moderation (8-12g per meal)
4. **Low GI Focus**: Prioritize  rated meals
5. **Budget-Friendly**: Most meals under ₹50 for vegetarian, ₹100 for non-vegetarian
6. **Prep Time**: Most meals under 40 minutes
7. **Spice Moderation**: Reduce if inflammation-sensitive
8. **Fermented Foods**: Include regularly for gut health
