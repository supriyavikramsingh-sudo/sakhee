### KETO SUBSTITUTES FOR PCOS-FRIENDLY MEAL PLANS ###
================================================================================

PURPOSE: Enable ketogenic (high-fat, low-carb) meal adaptations for PCOS management
TARGET MACROS: 70% fat, 25% protein, 5% carbs (typically 20-50g net carbs per day)
PCOS BENEFITS: Improved insulin sensitivity, reduced inflammation, hormone balance support, weight management
MEDICAL NOTE: Ketogenic diet should be discussed with healthcare provider, especially for those with other health conditions

#### GENERAL KETO PRINCIPLES ####
- Eliminate all grains (rice, wheat, oats, millets)
- Avoid starchy vegetables (potato, sweet potato, corn, peas)
- Limit legumes/lentils (high carb)
- Avoid most fruits (except small portions of berries)
- Eliminate sugar, jaggery, honey
- Focus on healthy fats: ghee, coconut oil, avocado oil, olive oil, butter
- Emphasize low-carb vegetables: leafy greens, cauliflower, zucchini, bell peppers, broccoli
- Include quality proteins: eggs, paneer, meat, fish, tofu
- Target net carbs: <50g/day (ideally 20-30g)
- Ensure adequate electrolytes: salt, potassium, magnesium
- Stay well-hydrated: 3-4 liters water daily

#### KETO + PCOS SYNERGY ####

BENEFITS FOR PCOS:
1. IMPROVED INSULIN SENSITIVITY: Low carb intake reduces insulin demand, improves insulin resistance
2. HORMONE BALANCE: Reduced insulin â†’ reduced androgen production â†’ improved hormone balance
3. WEIGHT MANAGEMENT: Fat burning (ketosis) supports weight loss, especially abdominal fat
4. REDUCED INFLAMMATION: Elimination of refined carbs and sugar reduces inflammatory markers
5. STABLE BLOOD SUGAR: Prevents glucose spikes and crashes, improves energy levels
6. IMPROVED LIPID PROFILE: May improve HDL, reduce triglycerides
7. REDUCED CRAVINGS: Stable blood sugar reduces carb/sugar cravings
8. MENSTRUAL REGULARITY: Improved insulin sensitivity can restore ovulation

KETO ADAPTATION PHASE:
- Week 1-2: "Keto flu" possible - fatigue, headache, irritability

- Week 3-4: Adaptation complete - energy increases, mental clarity improves
- Ongoing: Maintain electrolytes, hydration, fat intake

MEDICAL CONSIDERATIONS:
- Consult healthcare provider before starting keto, especially with:
  - Diabetes or prediabetes (may need medication adjustments)
  - Thyroid conditions
  - Kidney issues
  - History of eating disorders
  - Pregnancy or breastfeeding
- Monitor menstrual cycles - some women experience temporary irregularity initially
- Track symptoms and adjust as needed
- Consider working with nutritionist familiar with keto + PCOS

- Electrolytes: sodium, potassium, magnesium

- Track net carbs (total carbs - fiber)
