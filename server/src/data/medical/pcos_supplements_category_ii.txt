# PCOS SUPPLEMENT INFORMATION GUIDE

This guide provides evidence-based information about supplements that may support PCOS management. This information is for educational purposes only.

## IMPORTANT MEDICAL DISCLAIMERS

### Safety and Consultation Requirements

- This guide does NOT provide dosage recommendations
- Always consult a qualified healthcare professional before starting any supplement
- Your doctor will determine appropriate dosing based on your individual needs, lab values, and medical history
- Supplements are not a replacement for medical treatment or prescribed medications
- Individual responses vary significantly
- Quality and purity of supplements vary by manufacturer
- Inform your doctor about ALL supplements you take to avoid dangerous interactions
- Pregnant or breastfeeding women must consult their doctor before taking any supplements
- Get appropriate blood tests before starting certain supplements (e.g., Vitamin D levels)

### How to Use This Guide

- Identify your primary PCOS symptoms
- Review supplements that target those symptoms
- Note potential side effects and drug interactions
- Discuss with your healthcare provider before starting
- Your doctor will prescribe appropriate dosages



## CATEGORY II - EMERGING SCIENTIFIC SUPPORT

These supplements have some clinical evidence or strong anecdotal support but need more research for definitive recommendations.

### L-CARNITINE

**Category:** Emerging Evidence
**Evidence Level:** Moderate (Some clinical studies)
**Supplement Type:** Amino acid derivative, fat metabolism support

#### TARGET SYMPTOMS AND CONDITIONS
L-Carnitine may help with weight management and obesity, fatigue and low energy levels, insulin resistance, lipid metabolism issues, exercise performance, and fertility support.

#### HOW IT HELPS YOU
L-Carnitine facilitates the transport of fatty acids into your mitochondria where they can be burned for energy. This improves fat metabolism, insulin sensitivity, and cellular energy production. For women with PCOS who struggle with weight loss resistance, L-Carnitine may help support fat burning when combined with diet and exercise. Energy level improvements typically appear within two to four weeks. Weight management benefits, when combined with lifestyle changes, usually become noticeable after eight to twelve weeks. Metabolic improvements appear around six to eight weeks.

#### POTENTIAL SIDE EFFECTS
Some individuals produce a fishy body odor due to TMAO production, though this doesn't happen to everyone. Gastrointestinal distress including nausea, cramping, or diarrhea may occur. Restlessness or insomnia is possible if taken late in the day, so morning or afternoon is better.

#### DRUG INTERACTIONS AND IMPORTANT CAUTIONS
L-Carnitine may interfere with thyroid medications and function, so monitoring may be needed if you have thyroid issues. It may increase seizure risk in people taking anticonvulsants. It may enhance the effect of warfarin (blood thinner). Use caution with hypothyroidism. Take on an empty stomach or with carbohydrates for best absorption. The L-Carnitine L-Tartrate form is often preferred.

BEST FOR: May help with weight loss resistance common in PCOS. Supports energy production and fat metabolism. Can enhance exercise performance.

WHEN TO EXPECT RESULTS: Energy levels in two to four weeks, weight management in eight to twelve weeks with diet and exercise, metabolic improvements in six to eight weeks.

### N-ACETYL CYSTEINE (NAC)

**Category:** Emerging Evidence
**Evidence Level:** Moderate
**Supplement Type:** Amino acid, antioxidant precursor

#### TARGET SYMPTOMS AND CONDITIONS
NAC may help with ovulation induction, particularly as an adjunct to fertility treatment, antioxidant support and reducing oxidative stress, insulin resistance, high androgen levels, thinning hair, and respiratory issues.

#### HOW IT HELPS YOU
NAC is a precursor to glutathione, your body's master antioxidant. It reduces oxidative stress throughout your body, improves insulin sensitivity, and has mucolytic (mucus-thinning) properties. Some studies show NAC may help induce ovulation in women with PCOS, especially when combined with fertility medications like Clomid. Antioxidant benefits typically appear within four to six weeks. For ovulation induction, expect two to three menstrual cycles. Hair health improvements take three to six months.

#### POTENTIAL SIDE EFFECTS
Gastrointestinal discomfort including nausea, vomiting, or diarrhea is the most common side effect. NAC has a sulfur-like smell and taste that some find unpleasant. Rarely, skin rash or hives may occur. Headache is possible but uncommon.

#### DRUG INTERACTIONS AND IMPORTANT CAUTIONS
NAC may increase blood vessel dilation when combined with nitroglycerin, potentially causing headaches. It may increase bleeding risk when combined with blood thinners. Activated charcoal may reduce NAC effectiveness. In sensitive individuals with asthma, NAC may cause bronchospasm, so use with caution. Take on an empty stomach for best absorption. Can be combined with Vitamin C for enhanced antioxidant effect.

BEST FOR: Some studies show NAC may help induce ovulation in women with PCOS, especially when combined with fertility treatments. Good general antioxidant support.

WHEN TO EXPECT RESULTS: Antioxidant benefits in four to six weeks, ovulation induction in two to three cycles, hair health in three to six months.

### MAGNESIUM

**Category:** Emerging Evidence
**Evidence Level:** Moderate
**Supplement Type:** Mineral, enzyme cofactor

#### TARGET SYMPTOMS AND CONDITIONS
Magnesium may help with PMS symptoms including mood changes and cramping, sleep disturbances and insomnia, muscle cramps and tension, insulin resistance, anxiety and stress, and headaches or migraines.

#### HOW IT HELPS YOU
Magnesium is a cofactor in over 300 enzymatic reactions in your body, including those involved in insulin signaling, energy production, and neurotransmitter synthesis. It calms the nervous system and relaxes muscles. Many women with PCOS are magnesium deficient, and supplementation may improve insulin sensitivity, sleep quality, and stress management. Sleep improvements typically appear within one to two weeks. Muscle cramp relief also comes quickly, usually within one to two weeks. PMS symptom improvements may take one to two menstrual cycles. Insulin sensitivity changes take six to eight weeks.

#### POTENTIAL SIDE EFFECTS
The most common side effect is loose stools or diarrhea, especially with citrate and oxide forms. Nausea may occur. Abdominal cramping is possible. Very high amounts over long periods can cause magnesium toxicity, though this is rare with normal supplementation. These effects are usually mild and can be minimized by choosing the right form and taking with food.

#### DRUG INTERACTIONS AND IMPORTANT CAUTIONS
Diuretics may increase or decrease magnesium levels depending on the type, so monitoring may be needed. Magnesium reduces absorption of certain antibiotics like tetracyclines and quinolones, so take them at least two hours apart. Bisphosphonates for osteoporosis have reduced absorption when taken with magnesium. Proton pump inhibitors can reduce magnesium absorption over time. Use caution and close monitoring with kidney disease. Magnesium glycinate is gentler on the stomach and best for sleep. Citrate is good if you have constipation. Take in the evening for sleep support.

BEST FOR: Many women with PCOS are deficient. May improve insulin sensitivity, sleep quality, and stress management. Particularly helpful for PMS symptoms and muscle cramps.

WHEN TO EXPECT RESULTS: Sleep improvements in one to two weeks, muscle cramps in one to two weeks, PMS symptoms in one to two cycles, insulin sensitivity in six to eight weeks.

### ZINC

**Category:** Emerging Evidence
**Evidence Level:** Moderate
**Supplement Type:** Mineral, immune support

#### TARGET SYMPTOMS AND CONDITIONS
Zinc may help with acne and oily skin, hair loss and thinning, immune dysfunction and frequent infections, insulin resistance, poor wound healing, and reduced fertility.

#### HOW IT HELPS YOU
Zinc is essential for hormone production, immune function, protein synthesis, and insulin signaling. It has anti-androgenic and anti-inflammatory properties that can help with common PCOS symptoms. For women struggling with acne or hirsutism related to high androgens, zinc may provide relief. Immune function improvements typically appear within four to six weeks. Acne improvements take eight to twelve weeks. Hair health benefits take three to six months as hair growth is slow.

#### POTENTIAL SIDE EFFECTS
Nausea and stomach upset are common, especially if taken on an empty stomach. Metallic taste in the mouth may occur. Long-term use at high amounts can cause copper deficiency, which paradoxically reduces immune function. Headache is possible but uncommon.

#### DRUG INTERACTIONS AND IMPORTANT CAUTIONS
Zinc reduces absorption of iron supplements and vice versa, so take them at different times of day if you need both. Zinc reduces antibiotic absorption for tetracyclines and quinolones, so separate by at least two hours. It may interact with penicillamine used for rheumatoid arthritis. Thiazide diuretics may increase zinc loss. Take with food to reduce nausea. Zinc picolinate or glycinate forms are better absorbed. Don't exceed recommended amounts as excessive zinc can be harmful.

BEST FOR: Particularly helpful for women with acne and hirsutism related to high androgens. Supports immune health and may improve insulin sensitivity.

WHEN TO EXPECT RESULTS: Immune function in four to six weeks, acne improvements in eight to twelve weeks, hair health in three to six months.

### VITAMIN B COMPLEX (B6, B12, FOLATE)

**Category:** Emerging Evidence
**Evidence Level:** Moderate
**Supplement Type:** Vitamins, enzyme cofactors

#### TARGET SYMPTOMS AND CONDITIONS
B vitamins may help with fatigue and low energy levels, PMS and mood regulation, elevated homocysteine levels which increase cardiovascular risk, neurological health, methylation support, and Metformin-induced B12 deficiency which is very common.

#### HOW IT HELPS YOU
B vitamins are cofactors in energy metabolism, neurotransmitter synthesis, DNA synthesis, and methylation pathways. They're essential for cellular function and hormonal balance. For women taking Metformin, B12 supplementation is highly recommended as Metformin significantly reduces B12 absorption over time. Energy level improvements typically appear within one to two weeks. Mood improvements take four to six weeks. PMS symptom relief usually occurs after one to two cycles.

#### POTENTIAL SIDE EFFECTS
Bright yellow urine is common and harmless, caused by riboflavin (B2). Nausea may occur if taken on an empty stomach. Very high amounts of B6 (over 200mg) taken long-term may cause nerve irritation. Rarely, skin flushing may occur from niacin (B3).

#### DRUG INTERACTIONS AND IMPORTANT CAUTIONS
Metformin significantly reduces B12 absorption, making supplementation highly recommended for women taking this medication. Anticonvulsants may reduce folate levels. Proton pump inhibitors reduce B12 absorption over time. B6 may reduce the effectiveness of levodopa for Parkinson's disease. Look for methylated forms (methylcobalamin and methylfolate) for better absorption, especially if you have MTHFR gene variations. Take in the morning as B vitamins boost energy.

BEST FOR: Critical for women on Metformin, as it depletes B12. Supports energy production and hormonal balance. Folate is essential for women trying to conceive.

WHEN TO EXPECT RESULTS: Energy levels in one to two weeks, mood improvements in four to six weeks, PMS symptoms in one to two cycles.

### CINNAMON (CEYLON PREFERRED)

**Category:** Emerging Evidence
**Evidence Level:** Moderate
**Supplement Type:** Spice, polyphenol

#### TARGET SYMPTOMS AND CONDITIONS
Cinnamon may help with insulin resistance and high blood sugar, metabolic syndrome, poor glucose control, inflammation, and lipid imbalance.

#### HOW IT HELPS YOU
Cinnamon contains polyphenols that enhance insulin sensitivity, slow the breakdown of carbohydrates in your digestive system, and improve glucose uptake into cells. It can help with glucose control in insulin-resistant PCOS. Blood sugar improvements typically appear within two to four weeks. Insulin sensitivity improvements take six to eight weeks.

#### POTENTIAL SIDE EFFECTS
Cassia cinnamon, the common grocery store variety, can cause liver toxicity at high amounts with long-term use due to coumarin content. Mouth sores or allergic reactions may occur. When combined with diabetes medications, it may cause blood sugar to drop too low. Gastrointestinal irritation is possible.

#### DRUG INTERACTIONS AND IMPORTANT CAUTIONS
Cinnamon has additive blood sugar-lowering effects with diabetes medications. It may increase bleeding risk when combined with anticoagulants. Cassia cinnamon may stress the liver when combined with other liver medications. Avoid cassia cinnamon in pregnancy. Always choose Ceylon cinnamon (also called "true cinnamon") as it has much lower coumarin content and is safer for long-term use. Can be taken as powder, capsules, or added to food. Take with meals containing carbohydrates.

BEST FOR: May help with glucose control in insulin-resistant PCOS. Use Ceylon variety for safety with long-term use.

WHEN TO EXPECT RESULTS: Blood sugar improvements in two to four weeks, insulin sensitivity in six to eight weeks.

### SPEARMINT TEA / EXTRACT

**Category:** Emerging Evidence
**Evidence Level:** Moderate (for hirsutism)
**Supplement Type:** Herbal tea, anti-androgen

#### TARGET SYMPTOMS AND CONDITIONS
Spearmint may help with hirsutism including unwanted facial and body hair, high androgen levels particularly free testosterone, and acne related to androgen excess.

#### HOW IT HELPS YOU
Spearmint has anti-androgenic properties that can reduce free testosterone levels, potentially improving androgen-related symptoms like unwanted facial hair. It's one of the few natural anti-androgens with clinical evidence supporting its use. Hirsutism reduction takes three to six months as hair growth cycles are slow. Testosterone level reduction typically appears within one to three months.

#### POTENTIAL SIDE EFFECTS
Spearmint is generally very safe with no major side effects. Some people may experience mild gastrointestinal sensitivity. Rarely, heartburn or acid reflux may occur. Overall, it's one of the safest PCOS supplements.

#### DRUG INTERACTIONS AND IMPORTANT CAUTIONS
Spearmint may have additive effects with anti-androgen medications like spironolactone or finasteride. It may also have additive anti-androgen effects with hormonal birth control. Overall, it's safe for most people. Consistent daily use is required for benefits. Can be taken as tea (two cups daily brewed five to ten minutes) or as extract.

BEST FOR: One of the few natural anti-androgens with clinical evidence. Particularly helpful for women bothered by facial hair growth. Very safe for long-term use.

WHEN TO EXPECT RESULTS: Hirsutism reduction in three to six months (hair growth is slow), testosterone reduction in one to three months.

### PROBIOTICS (LACTOBACILLUS + BIFIDOBACTERIUM STRAINS)

**Category:** Emerging Evidence
**Evidence Level:** Moderate
**Supplement Type:** Beneficial bacteria

#### TARGET SYMPTOMS AND CONDITIONS
Probiotics may help with gut dysbiosis and digestive issues, chronic inflammation, insulin resistance through the gut-metabolism connection, mood imbalance via the gut-brain axis, immune dysfunction, and weight management.

#### HOW IT HELPS YOU
Probiotics restore healthy gut microbiome balance, reduce inflammation throughout your body, improve gut barrier function, and influence metabolic and hormonal signaling. Emerging research shows gut microbiome alterations in women with PCOS, and probiotics may improve insulin sensitivity and reduce inflammation. Digestive improvements typically appear within one to two weeks. Mood benefits take four to eight weeks. Metabolic effects appear around eight to twelve weeks.

#### POTENTIAL SIDE EFFECTS
Initial gas and bloating are common in the first one to two weeks as your gut adjusts to the new beneficial bacteria. Mild digestive discomfort may occur initially but usually resolves. Very rarely, infections may occur in severely immunocompromised individuals.

#### DRUG INTERACTIONS AND IMPORTANT CAUTIONS
Antibiotics kill probiotics along with harmful bacteria, so if you must take antibiotics, take probiotics two to three hours apart to minimize interaction. If you take immunosuppressants, consult your doctor before using probiotics. Generally safe for most people. Multi-strain formulas with both Lactobacillus and Bifidobacterium are preferred. Refrigerated and shelf-stable varieties are both available.

BEST FOR: Emerging research shows gut microbiome alterations in PCOS. Probiotics may improve insulin sensitivity and reduce inflammation. Supports digestive health and potentially mood.

WHEN TO EXPECT RESULTS: Digestive improvements in one to two weeks, mood benefits in four to eight weeks, metabolic effects in eight to twelve weeks.

### EVENING PRIMROSE OIL (EPO)

**Category:** Emerging Evidence
**Evidence Level:** Limited to Moderate
**Supplement Type:** Essential fatty acid (omega-6), source of GLA

#### TARGET SYMPTOMS AND CONDITIONS
EPO may help with PMS symptoms including breast tenderness and mood swings, irregular menstrual cycles, skin health issues like dryness or eczema, and general hormonal balance.

#### HOW IT HELPS YOU
Evening primrose oil contains gamma-linolenic acid (GLA), an omega-6 fatty acid that converts to anti-inflammatory compounds in your body and supports hormonal balance. Some women find it helpful for PMS symptoms, particularly when taken in the second half of the menstrual cycle. PMS improvements typically appear after one to two cycles. Skin health benefits take six to eight weeks.

#### POTENTIAL SIDE EFFECTS
Gastrointestinal upset, nausea, or diarrhea may occur. Headache is possible. Some people experience softening of stools.

#### DRUG INTERACTIONS AND IMPORTANT CAUTIONS
EPO may increase bleeding risk when combined with blood thinners. It may lower the seizure threshold in people with epilepsy, so avoid if you take seizure medications. It may interact with phenothiazine antipsychotic medications. Avoid in pregnancy as it may affect the uterus. Often taken in the luteal phase (second half) of the menstrual cycle for PMS support. Take with meals.

BEST FOR: May help with PMS symptoms and menstrual regularity in some women. Limited specific research for PCOS but may support hormonal balance.

WHEN TO EXPECT RESULTS: PMS improvements in one to two cycles, skin health in six to eight weeks.
